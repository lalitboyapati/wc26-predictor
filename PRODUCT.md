# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Bettors and analysts following the 2026 World Cup who want to dig into win probabilities, expected goals, lineup breakdowns, and betting angles to make informed decisions. They approach the app with intent — they're not browsing casually, they're looking for a specific signal (Who's favoured? What's the xG? Which prop is worth a look?). Secondary audience: developers and designers viewing this as a portfolio/technical showcase.

## Product Purpose

A fully client-side World Cup 2026 predictor and analytics app. Computes win/draw/loss probabilities, expected goals, projected group standings, a seeded knockout bracket, and position-specific player prop suggestions — all from static data, no backend. A simulated tournament clock makes matches go live and finish over a session. The app is a portfolio piece as much as a functional tool: the quality of execution is the statement.

## Brand Personality

Precise, authoritative, restrained. The design should feel like a premium sports analytics platform (Opta, ESPN Stats & Info) — not a betting site, not a fan app. Data is the product; the interface should make it legible and credible.

## Anti-references

- **Generic sports-app blue** — the default navy/royal-blue palette that reads "sports" before any content is visible. The current gold-on-near-black identity avoids this and should be preserved.
- **Casino / gambling aesthetic** — neon colors, urgency cues, aggressive CTAs, flashing elements. The betting-suggestion feature should be presented with the same analytic restraint as the rest of the data, not promoted as gambling.

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns the surface.** Every visual element should make the data clearer or more actionable. No decoration that doesn't carry information.
2. **Confidence without noise.** Authoritative and precise, never exciting or urgent. The model has opinions; the UI states them plainly.
3. **Show the model, don't hide it.** Predictions are probabilistic estimates, not facts. Communicate uncertainty explicitly (probability bars, percentage labels) rather than presenting outcomes as certainties.
4. **Showcase through craft.** This is also a portfolio piece. Quality of execution — typography, spacing, motion, edge-case handling — is the differentiator, not marketing copy.
5. **Betting-aware, not betting-obsessed.** Odds and prop suggestions are analytical tools presented with the same restraint as win probability. Never make the app feel like a sportsbook.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target. Functional keyboard nav and reasonable contrast are expected as baseline craft; no formal audit is planned.
